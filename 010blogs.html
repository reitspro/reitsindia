REITs versus direct real estate investing

REITs and direct real estate investing

REIT evaluation by comparison with direct real estate investing

A comparison of commercial real estate investing is taken up here, since REITs are mainly about CRE investing. Home ownership is not focused on here.

Positives of REIT investments: It has a very low ticket size. Anyone with Rs 500 or below can invest in REITs. Its easy to invest as your stock trading website or app offers them like regular stock. You don’t need to search for properties and also need not manage properties. One does away with legal hassles also. Similarly, they are easy to sell. Also brokerage is low. Lastly REITcan give you geographical diversity because your investment represents a large set of properties.

Negatives of REITs are:. Since REITs pay high dividends they don’t increase much in price over a time period. Also, you don’t have any direct control over your investment and the REIT fund decides what property to buy and sell. Lastly, you cannot directly use leverage to amplify returns or take tax benefits.

Direct Real Estate positives are: You can get loans and make money on your property in upswings. A REIT also invests in leveraged properties and you own a portion of that but the impact of leveraging is not really amplified as in direct real estate investment. Also there is lot of control on property, you get an emotional satisfaction / pride of ownership. In residential property investment, tax breaks are there for Indian investors (home owners).

Investment in Real Estate negative: Bulk payment, Illiquid secondary market, hassles in finding the right deal with proper legal titles, managing a rental property. And exiting the investment.

Conclusively:

For retail investors, Mutual Funds and Pension Funds, REITs make a lot of sense due to some comparative advantages
